Questions, answered.

Does Kindred work on iPhone and Android?

Yes — it’s a native app on both, with everything kept in sync across your household’s phones. iPad and Android tablets are supported in portrait; landscape is on the way.

Is there a free version?

Yes. The free tier runs one shared household with unlimited meals, shopping lists, chores and recipes — no credit card, no time limit. Premium only adds the AI extras (meal planning, recipe extraction and images) and priority sync.

Do my partner or family need their own subscriptions?

No. One Premium covers your whole household. Invite anyone you live with — they sign in free and you pay once. It’s built for 2–4 people sharing a home.

How does the AI meal planner work?

Tap Plan Next Week and Kindred reads what’s already in your stock, what your household has actually eaten, and any dietary notes you’ve set — then drafts a week. You approve it, shuffle a night, or regenerate. If a draft fails or you don’t like it, the AI credit comes back.

Is my household data private?

Yes. Your data is yours, stored on Google Cloud in the UK/EU region. We don’t sell it, we don’t show ads against it, and we don’t use it to train AI. The full details are in our Privacy Policy.

Do you use my data to train AI?

No. Your household’s meals, lists and notes are never used to train any AI model. The AI features read your data only to draft your own week or tidy a recipe you’ve added — nothing leaves to train a model.

What happens to my data if I cancel?

Nothing is lost. Your meals, lists, recipes and chores stay in your account. You simply drop to Free — the full app minus the AI extras. Re-subscribe any time and the AI features switch back on with your data intact.

Can I cancel any time?

Yes — through the App Store or Google Play, in one tap. No retention call, no email loops, no hoops. You keep the free app for as long as you like.
